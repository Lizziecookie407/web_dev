What the freak is it?

Capitol Reef is located in south-central Utah. it is known for its unique geology, rich cultural history, and spectacular dark night skies. It includes the Waterpocket Fold, which is a a nearly 100-mile-long geologic monocline in the Earth's crust. It exposes layers of rock that are 200 million years old. Why should I even go?

The park is open year-round and has many differnt things to do.

  • Hickman Bridge Trail: 1.7 miles leading to a natural bridge
  • Cassidy Arch Trail: 3.2 miles leading to the top of an arch
  • Grand Wash: about 4.7 miles through narrow canyon walls
  • Cohab Canyon Trail: great views of the Fruita area and the Waterpocket Fold
  • Backpacking and Canyoneering, permits are required to stay overnight
  • Biking, Horseback Riding, and Rock Climbing, only in designated areas

This is essentially a colossal, petrified wrinkle in the Earth's crust. The Waterpocket Fold is kind of the backbone of Capitol Reef. It's name come from the small basins, or “waterpockets,” that form in the sandstone as it erodes. When rainwater collects in the tiny pools, it's actually a very improtant part of the park's ecosystem.
